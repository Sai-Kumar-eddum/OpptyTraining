ABOUT THE EVENT

The Legacy of Our Campus Flagship
Euphoria 2026 is the official annual national-level cultural festival of our institution. Entirely conceptualized, organized, and executed by the student community, this mega-event stands as the crown jewel of our academic calendar. For three decades, our campus has thrown open its gates to welcome premium talent from colleges and universities nationwide, creating an elite arena where academic dedication meets artistic brilliance.
Where Campus Life Meets Creative Freedom
This event serves as a crucial bridge between standard classroom education and real-world leadership. Through managing massive logistics, massive budgets, and cross-country marketing campaigns, our student committees bring this cultural city to life. For the participants, it is a high-stakes competitive platform to test their skills against the absolute best minds in the country. We strip away the textbook boundaries to let you express, compete, and network. Step onto our campus grounds this August and feel the cultural wave.

1. Resonance: The National Battle of the Bands

Event Overview

Resonance is the flagship musical showdown of Euphoria 2026, bringing together the fiercest college rock, metal, and fusion bands from across the country. This high-decibel competition is designed to test raw musicality, stage presence, and tight sonic cohesion under professional stadium lighting. It is not just about playing loud; it is about captivating a massive, high-energy crowd and convincing a panel of veteran industry judges that your band owns the stage.

Performance & Equipment Guidelines

  • Time Constraints: Each band receives 20 minutes total on stage, including a maximum of 5 minutes for soundcheck and 15 minutes for actual performance. Exceeding time limits results in automatic point deductions.
  • Track Requirements: Bands must perform at least one original composition and one re-arranged cover song to showcase both creativity and technical adaptation.
  • Provided Backline: The festival provides a standard professional drum kit, dual guitar amplifiers, a bass amplifier, and vocal microphones.
  • Personal Gear: Musicians must bring their own guitars, bass instruments, drum breakables (cymbals, snare, kick pedal), and special effects pedals.

2. Choreo-Wars: The Mega Group Dance Championship

Event Overview

Choreo-Wars is a high-octane celebration of rhythm, synchronization, and athletic storytelling. This flagship dance competition invites elite collegiate dance troupes to unleash their creativity on a massive open-air stage. Troupes must blend intricate footwork, explosive formations, and deep thematic concepts into a unified performance that keeps the audience on the edge of their seats.

Performance & Equipment Guidelines

  • Team Size: Groups must consist of a minimum of 8 and a maximum of 25 active dancers on stage at any given time.
  • Time Duration: The performance duration must be between 6 and 10 minutes. Timing begins immediately with the first audio cue.
  • Audio Submission: Soundtracks must be submitted in high-quality .mp3 or .wav format to the technical committee via the website portal 48 hours before the event. Teams must also carry a backup USB drive.
  • Props Policy: Use of hand-held props is highly encouraged but must be cleared by safety coordinators. Liquids, fire, powders, and hazardous materials are strictly banned.

3. Nukkad Natak: Street Play for Social Change

Event Overview

Nukkad Natak strips away modern technology to celebrate raw, acoustic human drama. Held in the open campus courtyards, this event challenges student theater societies to use powerful voices, heavy synchronization, and traditional instruments to spotlight critical, real-world social issues. Without a stage or spotlights, actors rely on intense crowd interaction, emotional depth, and booming vocal projection to turn casual bystanders into a captivated audience.

Performance & Equipment Guidelines

  • Team Boundaries: Teams can comprise 10 to 20 members, including instrumentalists who provide live background scores.
  • Time Constraints: Plays must run for a minimum of 15 minutes and a maximum of 25 minutes. A warning bell rings at the 20-minute mark.
  • Acoustic Restrictions: Electronic microphones, speakers, amplifiers, and pre-recorded audio tracks are strictly prohibited. All sound must be live.
  • Permitted Instruments: Teams are encouraged to use traditional acoustic instruments such as Dholaks, Dafils, Flutes, and harmoniums to elevate their storytelling.
