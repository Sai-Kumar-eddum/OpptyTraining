Earth:

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only known celestial body to support life, characterized by its vast liquid oceans and a life-sustaining atmosphere rich in nitrogen and oxygen(O2). Formed approximately 4.54 billion years ago, it possesses a complex internal structure consisting of a solid iron-nickel inner core, a liquid outer core, a rocky mantle, and a thin outer crust broken into shifting tectonic plates. The planet's unique position in the "Goldilocks zone" allows for stable temperatures that keep water in liquid form, while its magnetic field shields it from harmful solar radiation. Beyond its physical properties, Earth is a dynamic system where the geosphere, hydrosphere, and atmosphere interact to support millions of species, making it a singular and fragile oasis in the vastness of the solar system.
